Evaluation

My Rating of This Book

In my opinion, I believe this was a pretty great book. However, it has it's faults. Overall I would rate it a 6.78 out of 10. It would be rated A LOT higher if it weren't for the first part, Akhmin. Oh for the love of God, Ahkmin! Without it, I would rate it a solid 8.7. I will tell you why. Ahkmin was very boring. Most of it was her when she was, like, 5 years old. It was so boring and uneventful that I didn't even like reading it. I thought, ok, I'll go a little more to see if it picks up. And pick up it did. It got exciting and interesting the exact moment she got to Thebes. I liked the love triangle with Nefertiti, Amenophis, and Thutmose. This sounds weird, but I liked the politics a lot, too. I thought it was cool how it was like a slice of life for Egyptians. It was a nice brake from Demigods being blasted out of Mt. St. Helen's after getting lava thrown on them by sea demons and ending up on the Island of Eclypso (even though that's still epic). It's like the opposite of Sunrise Over Fellujah. I think Esther Friesner should have made her home town Abydos. In conclusion, the good outweighs the bad, so I'll meet them in the middle and give it a solid 7.48 Egyptian ankhs out of 10 possible ankhs.
